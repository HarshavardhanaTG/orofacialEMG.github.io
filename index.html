Geometry of orofacial neuromuscular signals: speech articulation decoding using surface electromyography

Harshavardhana T. Gowda1
Zachary D. McNaughton1
Lee M. Miller1
1University of California, Davis
Paper Code Data

Abstract

In this work, we provide the first systematic investigation of the structure of orofacial EMG signals. We collect data from a large cohort of individuals as they perform orofacial movements and articulate English phonemes and words.

We show that the manifold of symmetric positive definite (SPD) matrices provides a natural embedding space for EMG signals. Within this space, EMG activity can be approximately represented as a linear combination of orthogonal basis vectors. We further show that distribution shift across individuals can be interpreted as a change of basis in this underlying EMG signal space.

Our results demonstrate that orofacial EMG signals can span the entire phonemic space of the English language, supporting their use as a rich neuromuscular representation for speech. We also introduce a general language-spelling interface based on NATO phonetic codes.

Together, these findings establish a foundational understanding of the data structure of orofacial EMG signals. This work serves as the basis for our subsequent studies demonstrating direct EMG-to-text and EMG-to-speech decoding, which are linked below.
